# HR-Analytics-Dashboard-PowerBI
Interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing employee attrition patterns across 736 employees, identifying key retention risk factors and providing data-driven insights for HR strategy

## Overview
This Power BI dashboard provides comprehensive analysis of employee attrition patterns across 736 employees. The project identifies key retention risk factors and delivers actionable insights to reduce the current 18.48% attrition rate through data-driven decision making.

## Problem Statement
Employee turnover creates significant organizational costs including recruitment expenses, training investments, and lost productivity. This analysis aims to identify attrition patterns and high-risk employee segments to develop targeted retention strategies that can save millions in turnover costs.

## Key Insights

### 1. Compensation-Driven Attrition
- 72.8% of attrition (99 cases) occurs in the lowest salary bracket (Upto 5K)
- Higher salary slabs show 60-70% reduction in attrition rates
- Potential savings of $1.5M+ annually through targeted compensation adjustments

### 2. Age Group Risk Analysis
- 26-35 age bracket shows highest attrition with 331 employees (45% of workforce)
- This group shows 243% more attrition than the next highest bracket
- Represents peak productivity years requiring focused retention programs

### 3. Role-Specific Vulnerability
- Laboratory Technicians demonstrate highest attrition (45 cases)
- Sales Executives follow with 30 cases
- Research Scientists show 28 attrition cases
- These three roles account for 76% of total role-based attrition

### 4. Tenure Pattern Analysis
- Peak attrition occurs at 1-year mark (36 employees)
- Secondary spike at 5-year milestone (12 employees)
- Indicates critical intervention points for onboarding and career development

### 5. Education Background Impact
- Life Sciences graduates show 21.02% of total attrition
- Medical background employees contribute 15.71%
- Marketing and Technical degrees show lower attrition rates

### 6. Gender Disparity
- Male employees show 136 attrition cases vs 76 female cases
- Represents 79% higher attrition among male employees
- Requires investigation into engagement and development opportunities

## Business Impact
- Identified 72.8% of attrition concentrated in lowest salary bracket, enabling targeted compensation strategy
- Mapped 331 high-risk employees in critical 26-35 age demographic for focused retention programs
- Pinpointed Laboratory Technician role requiring immediate career development intervention
- Established critical intervention timing at 1-year and 5-year tenure marks
- Projected $1.5M+ annual savings through strategic attrition reduction initiatives

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-3 Months)
1. Implement 15-20% salary adjustment for lowest bracket to address 72.8% of attrition
2. Launch Laboratory Technician career development program (45 at-risk employees)
3. Enhance first-year onboarding experience to reduce 36 high-risk cases

### Medium-Term Initiatives (3-6 Months)
1. Design age-targeted engagement programs for 26-35 demographic
2. Create role-specific advancement pathways for high-attrition positions
3. Implement stay interview program for identified high-risk profiles

### Long-Term Strategy (6-12 Months)
1. Develop predictive attrition model for early risk identification
2. Conduct comprehensive compensation benchmarking across all roles
3. Address gender disparity through culture and opportunity analysis
4. Redesign employee value proposition aligned with market expectations
